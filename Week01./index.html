How to make Onigiri!

What is Onigiri?

Onigiri, also called O-musubi or Nigirimeshi are Japanese Rice Balls that are hand shaped
into round balls or triangles and usually wrapped in nori (seaweed). The most delicious and
quick snack or lunch to go.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of short grain rice
  • roasted seaweed/nori sheets
  • salt
  • 1–2 tsp Yukari Shiso
  • 1–2 tsp Vegan Vegetable Furikake
  • Umeboshi (Japanese pickled plums) or filling of your choice

Instructions

  1. Cook your rice and let it rest for 5-10 minutes in the rice cooker.
  2. While letting the rice rest, have a small bowl of salt, water, furikake and your fillings ready to be used.
  3. Place some rice into a medium size bowl and mix some salt in to taste with a rice paddle.
  4. Scoop around 1/3 or 1/2 cup of rice in the middle of your hand and gently press it in your palm.
  5. Make a small indent in the middle and add 1 pitted umeboshi or 1/2-1 tbsp of filling.
  6. Still holding the rice ball, curl your hand and slowly cover the filling with rice from the side.
  7. Shape it into a ball or rounded triangle by gently pressing between your two hands
  8. Place onto a tray or container and cover with a damp cloth. Repeat until you make as many as you desire.
  9. If eating immediately, wrap your rice balls with the roasted cut up nori sheets and serve.(Optional)
